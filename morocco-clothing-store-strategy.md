## 2. Why This Works

- **Low ad costs**: Morocco has among the lowest Facebook ad costs globally. $200-300 tests a product thoroughly.
- **COD preference**: 65-80% pay cash on delivery -- no payment gateway friction, no chargebacks.
- **Local brand preference**: 62% of Moroccans prefer local brands over global ones (Ipsos 2024).
- **Social commerce is huge**: 49% shop via social media pages, 32% order through messaging apps.
- **Underserved premium-local niche**: Most clothing stores on social media sell cheap, unbranded goods. Almost no one has built a **branded** premium-local streetwear brand.
- **Momentum**: E-commerce is growing 25%+ annually, and Ramadan alone saw 13% GMV growth.

## 3. Recommended Niche: Moroccan Urban Essentials

**Not flashy logos. Minimalist premium basics with Moroccan identity.**

Examples: quality plain/lightly-branded t-shirts, hoodies, sweatshirts, caps, tote bags in neutral/earth tones with subtle Moroccan patterns or Arabic calligraphy.

**Why this niche:**
- Basics never go out of style (no trend risk)
- Huge margin potential ($3-5 cost → $25-40 selling price)
- Easy to source locally (Derb Omar, Casablanca) or from Turkey (5-7 day shipping)
- Minimal sizing issues vs. structured clothing (fewer returns)
- Instagram aesthetic fits perfectly

**Target customer**: 18-34 year old urban Moroccan, style-conscious, uses Instagram/TikTok daily, wants to look premium but can't afford international brands like Nike, Carhartt, Essentials Fear of God ($80-150+).

**Your positioning**: The affordable premium local alternative. "European quality, Moroccan soul."

## 4. Product Selection & Unified Pricing

Every product is **199 DH** flat. No premium pricing, no tiers. One price for everything.

### Pricing Model

| Quantity | Total Price | Savings |
|---|---|---|
| 1 item | 199 DH | -- |
| 2 items | 279 DH (139.50/item) | Save 119 DH |
| 3 items | 349 DH (116.33/item) | Save 248 DH |

### Why This Pricing Wins
- **One number to remember**: 199 DH. No "was X now Y" confusion.
- **Volume incentive**: The jump from 199 → 279 for 2 items is irresistible. Customer thinks "just 80 more DH for a second item."
- **Margin protection**: 3 for 349 DH still gives 50%+ margin at 116 DH/item.
- **No discounting**: The price IS the price. No coupons, no sales, no promo codes.
- **Upsells at full price**: Every cross-sell and upsell is at the standard 199 DH. No bundle discounts needed -- the multi-buy pricing already handles volume.

### Product Selection (Start Small)

|---|---|---|---|

**Note on hoodies**: At 199 DH with 100-150 DH cost, margin is low (25-33%). Accept this. Hoodies drive traffic and AOV; caps/totes/socks recover margin. At 2+ items per order, blended margin stays healthy.

## 5. Sourcing

**Option A: Local (Derb Omar, Casablanca) -- RECOMMENDED**
- Visit Derb Omar wholesale market in Casablanca
- Find plain high-quality blanks (t-shirts, hoodies) at wholesale
- Work with a local screen printer/embroiderer for branding
- Shipping: 24-48h delivery anywhere in Morocco
- Lower return rates, easier quality control, no customs
- Pay cash directly, build relationships

**Option B: Turkey**
- Turkish cotton hoodies/sweatshirts are high quality
- 5-7 day shipping via cargo
- Better for "heavy" premium feel items

**Option C: China (via agent)**
- Lowest cost, but 15-25 day shipping
- Quality inconsistency
- Use only if you have zero budget and need absolute lowest cost

## 6. The Critical Piece: Premium Packaging (Justifies High Prices)

This is how you sell a 50 MAD t-shirt for 250 MAD. The unboxing experience IS the product.

### Packaging Kit (per order):

| Item | Est. Cost (MAD) | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Rigid magnetic closure box (custom print) | 15-25 | First impression -- feels premium instantly |
| Tissue paper with brand logo/stamp | 2-3 | Layers of discovery |
| Branded sticker seal (wax or gold foil) | 2 | Tamper-evident, premium touch |
| Cotton dust bag (for hoodies) | 5-8 | Luxury signal, reusable |
| Care card (thick cardstock, brand design) | 1-2 | Reduces returns, builds trust |
| Handwritten-style thank you note | 1 | Personal connection |
| Signature scent strip (light fragrance) | 1 | Olfactory memory = brand recall |
| **Total packaging cost** | **~27-40 MAD** | |

**Unit economics (t-shirt single item):**
- Product: 50 DH
- Packaging: 35 DH
- Delivery: 25 DH
- Marketing: 30 DH
- **Total cost**: ~140 DH
- **Selling price**: 199 DH
- **Profit**: 59 DH per item (30% margin on single)

**Unit economics (3 items per order):**
- 3 t-shirts cost: 150 DH
- Packaging: 35 DH (shared)
- Delivery: 25 DH (shared)
- Marketing: 60 DH (shared)
- **Total cost**: ~270 DH
- **Selling price**: 349 DH
- **Profit**: 79 DH per order (23% margin but higher AOV)

**The real profit is in the mix**: 
- 1 hoodie (cost 130 DH) + 1 cap (cost 30 DH) at 279 DH for 2
- Cost: ~240 DH with packaging/delivery → 39 DH profit (thin but builds customer base)
- 1 tee (50) + 1 cap (30) + 1 socks (20) at 349 DH for 3
- Cost: ~220 DH → 129 DH profit (37% margin)

At 10 orders/day (avg 2 items) = ~1,100-2,000 DH/day profit
At 30 orders/day = ~3,300-6,000 DH/day profit
At 100 orders/day = ~11,000-20,000 DH/day profit

### Packaging Psychology
- **Heavy = premium**: Use thick cardstock, rigid boxes, weighty materials
- **Layers = anticipation**: Customers should unwrap multiple layers (box → tissue → dust bag → product)
- **Scent = memory**: A signature scent creates brand recall stronger than visuals
- **Shareable = free marketing**: Design the box so customers WANT to post it on Instagram

## 8. Website Architecture (Next.js + React -- Zero Monthly Cost)

### Tech Stack

| Component | Choice | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Framework | Next.js 14+ (App Router) | Free |
| Hosting | Vercel free tier | Free |
| Styling | Tailwind CSS | Free |
| RTL Fonts | Cairo (Arabic) + Inter (numbers) from Google Fonts | Free |
| Icons | Tabler Icons or Lucide React | Free |
| Orders DB | Google Sheets + Google Apps Script webhook | Free |
| Analytics | Google Analytics 4 | Free |
| Domain | `.ma` domain (e.g., assil.ma) | ~300 MAD/year |
| **Total monthly** | **0 MAD** | |

### Why This Stack

- **No subscriptions**: Your only cost is the domain. No Shopify ($39/mo), no WooCommerce hosting ($10-20/mo), no apps.
- **Static generation (SSG)**: Product pages are pre-built HTML -- load instantly, zero server cost, unlimited traffic.
- **Client-side cart**: All cart logic runs in the browser. No database needed for browsing.
- **Google Sheets as backend**: Each order = one row in a Sheet. Free, real-time, accessible from phone.
- **COD only**: No payment gateway fees. No Stripe, no PayPal. Form just collects name + phone.

### How Google Sheets Handles Orders

1. Customer fills checkout form (name + phone)
2. Next.js POSTs JSON to a Google Apps Script webhook URL
3. Apps Script appends a row to your Google Sheet
4. You get an email notification (Sheet's built-in trigger)
5. You check the Sheet daily to see new orders: who ordered what, their phone, their city

**Google Sheet columns**: Timestamp | Order ID | Products (JSON) | Total DH | Customer Name | Phone | Multi-Buy Tier | Upsell Added (true/false) | Status | Notes

### 9.5 Cross-Sell & Upsell Strategy (All at 199 DH Each)

**All cross-sells and upsells are at the standard 199 DH price. No bundle discounts.** The multi-buy (2 for 279, 3 for 349) is the only discount mechanism and auto-applies in cart.

---

#### Touchpoint 1: Upsell Timer Modal (10-15 seconds)

**When it triggers**: Immediately after any "أضف للسلة" click (product page or collection).

**What it shows**: ONE product only. The most logical upsell based on current cart contents:

| If cart has | Show upsell | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| T-shirt | Cap | Complete the look, highest attach rate |
| Hoodie | T-shirt | Most logical pairing |
| Cap | Socks (pack) | Lowest price gap |
| Tote bag | Cap | Accessory + accessory |
| Nothing (first add) | Hero product (e.g., bestseller tee) | Push toward multi-buy |

**Timer behavior**:
- Modal slides in with product image, name, "زيدها لـ 199 DH فقط"
- A circular countdown timer (10-15 seconds) at the top of the modal
- Button: "أضف للسلة - 199 DH" (Add to cart)
- Below button: small "لا، شكراً" (No thanks) link
- If timer runs out: modal auto-closes, item was NOT added
- If user clicks "أضف": product is added to cart, modal shows "تمت الإضافة ✓" briefly then closes
- If user clicks "لا، شكراً": modal closes immediately, item was NOT added

**Key rule**: The upsell item is ONLY recorded if the user clicks "أضف" before the timer ends. If skipped or timed out, the order only contains the original items. This is critical for the thank you page logic.

**Timer duration**: 10 seconds on repeat visitors, 15 seconds on first visit (customers need more time to decide initially).

---

#### Touchpoint 2: Product Page Bottom

- 3 products, each with "199 DH" + "أضف" button

**Form fields**:
1. **الاسم الكامل** (Full name) -- text input, required, min 3 chars
2. **رقم الهاتف** (Phone) -- tel input, required, validation:
   - Must start with 05, 06, or 07
   - Must be exactly 10 digits
   - Auto-format as: 06 XX XX XX XX (spaces after every 2 digits)
   - Example shown below: مثال: 0612345678
   - Validation message (if wrong): "الرجاء إدخال رقم هاتف مغربي صحيح (06/07/05)"

**Upsell tracking in form submission**: The order payload sent to Google Sheets includes:
- `items`: Array of all products the customer added (original + upsell if they clicked "أضف")
- `upsellAdded`: boolean (true if they clicked the upsell within the timer, false if skipped/timed out)
- `upsellSkipped`: boolean (true if they clicked "لا، شكراً" or let timer expire)

This lets you track: "what % of customers accept the upsell?" and adjust the product/timer accordingly.

**No address field at checkout** -- COD confirmation call will get the address. Less friction = higher conversion.

**Checkout CRO elements**:
- Order summary on left (persistent, always visible)
- Trust text near submit: "سيتم الاتصال بك لتأكيد الطلب قبل التوصيل" (You'll be called to confirm before delivery)
- "الدفع عند التسليم" badge with cash icon
- Total shows: السعر + التوصيل = المجموع
- Submit button: full width, brand color, "تأكيد الطلب" with arrow
- Back link: "الرجوع للسلة"

**Form states**:
- **Default**: Empty fields, example visible
- **Validating**: Real-time as user types (phone format check)
- **Submitting**: Button shows spinner + "جاري تأكيد الطلب..."
- **Error**: Inline red text below the invalid field
- **Network error**: Toast "حدث خطأ. الرجاء المحاولة مرة أخرى" with retry

**Page receives data**: Order ID, items ordered, whether upsell was added or skipped, customer name.

**Layout**:

1. **Success hero**:
   - Green checkmark icon (large)
   - "تم تأكيد طلبك يا {{name}}! 🎉"
   - Order ID: #0001
   
2. **Delivery trust**:
   - Timeline: "منتظرينك ف 3 - 5 أيام" (Expect it in 3-5 days)
   - Stat: "أكثر من 92% من الطلبات توصل في الوقت المحدد" (92% of orders arrive on time)
   - What happens next: "غادي نتواصلو معاك فالتلفون باش نأكدو الطلب وناخدو العنوان ديالك" (We'll call you to confirm and get your address)
   
3. **Order summary**:
   - **If upsell was added**: Shows original items + upsell item with "199 DH" pricing, multi-buy total
   - **If upsell was skipped**: Shows only original items
   - Summary is read-only, matching what was sent to the Google Sheet
   
4. **Smart cross-sell section** (depends on what was ordered and upsell status):
   - **If customer did NOT add the upsell** (clicked "لا، شكراً" or timer expired):
     - "مازال الوقت! زيد هاد لـ 199 DH" (Still time! Add this for 199 DH)
     - Shows the SAME product that was in the upsell timer
     - "طلب دابا" (Order Now) → creates a NEW separate order for that item
   - **If customer DID add the upsell**:
     - "هادو كايديرو مع طلبك" (These go with your order)
     - 3 different products at 199 DH, each with "طلب دابا"
   - All cross-sells on thank you are at **full 199 DH price** and create NEW orders (not added to current)
   
5. **Social proof**: "انضم لـ +500 زبون واثق فينا"
   
6. **Share**: "شارك الطلب مع صاحبك" -- WhatsApp share with pre-filled message

**180 IQ Thank You CRO**:
- The thank you page is a SECOND conversion opportunity, not just a receipt
- If they skipped the upsell, the thank you page gives them a SECOND chance to buy it
- If they accepted the upsell, thank you shows different products for new orders
- Delivery confidence stat reduces buyer's remorse → higher delivery acceptance rate
- Every cross-sell is a separate order (no complicating the current one)

## 14. Problems to Expect & Solutions

| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| Google Sheets webhook down | Webhook is called once. If it fails, show "حدث خطأ" and let user retry. The form is not lost on error (data persisted in cart). |
| Phone validation false negatives | Moroccan numbers can be: 06XX, 07XX, 05XX. Show accepted formats: مثال: 0612345678 or 0712345678. Strip spaces before validation. |
| Cart lost on page refresh | Store cart in localStorage. Hydrate on mount. |
| RTL alignment bugs | Use Tailwind RTL variants: `text-right rtl:text-right ltr:text-left`. Test every component. |
| Images load slowly on mobile | Use Next.js `<Image>` component with `priority` on hero image, lazy load others. |
| Customer enters wrong phone | No server-side validation possible without SMS. Accept the risk. Call to confirm catches mistakes. |
| Order confirmation rate low at first | Call every customer yourself. Personal touch beats automation at small scale. |
| Cross-sells don't convert | Make them contextual (only show matching products). Use bundle pricing (half price). Test which positions convert best. |

## 15. Risk Mitigation

- **Zero monthly cost**: Even if the store gets 0 orders, you owe nothing. Only domain (~300 MAD/year).
- **No payment gateway**: COD means no chargebacks, no gateway fees, no integration complexity.
- **Small inventory risk**: Source 30-50 units per SKU. Max loss if nothing sells: 3,000-5,000 MAD.
- **Google Sheets is not a real database**: For launch it's fine. If you hit 100+ orders/day, migrate to Airtable (free tier) or a headless CMS.
- **No WhatsApp integration**: For now, confirm orders by calling. At 10+ orders/day, add WhatsApp manually. Don't automate until you understand the flow.
